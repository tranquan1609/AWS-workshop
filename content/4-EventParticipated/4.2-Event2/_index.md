# Summary Report: “First Cloud AI Journey – Career Development & AWS Skills Event”

### Event Objectives

- Share learning paths and AWS skill development through Cloud Quest and Floci platforms.
- Introduce real experiences from Hackathons and building products in a short timeframe.
- Strengthen soft skills, especially confidence in learning, communication, and work.
- Share the role of DevOps in modern software development and lessons from real projects.

### Speakers

- **Huynh Thai Linh** — *Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci*
- **The Ballers** (Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Minh Quan) — *Hackathon: "HA! a tons of fun"*
- **Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** — *Why We Always Need Confidence*
- **Tran Minh Quan** — *The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster*

### Key Highlights

#### Level Up Your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci

- Introduced AWS Cloud Quest and Floci — gamified learning platforms for AWS knowledge.
- Shared AWS learning paths for students and beginners, from fundamentals to popular AWS services.
- Guided how to use free learning resources, hands-on labs, and prepare for AWS certifications.
- Shared experience building Cloud Computing and AI foundations for future industry needs.

#### Hackathon – "HA! a tons of fun"

- Shared Hackathon experience from ideation to product completion.
- Introduced task division among team members to leverage individual strengths.
- Discussed challenges: time limits, bugs during development, and deadline pressure.
- Lessons on teamwork, communication, time management, and problem-solving under pressure.

#### Why We Always Need Confidence

- Analyzed the role of confidence in learning, work, and life.
- Shared methods to overcome anxiety in communication, presentations, and interviews.
- Encouraged students to ask questions, learn proactively, and seize new opportunities.
- Emphasized that confidence is built through practice and real experience, not innate talent.

#### The Hidden Iceberg of a Project: DevOps Before Disaster

- Introduced DevOps in modern software development and operations.
- Analyzed "hidden" project factors often overlooked: Source Control, CI/CD, Automated Testing, Monitoring & Logging, Infrastructure Automation, Security.
- Shared why projects fail despite working well during development.
- Emphasized building DevOps processes early for stability and scalability.

### Key Takeaways

#### Learning skills

- Understanding AWS learning paths for students and beginners.
- Using Cloud Quest and Floci for theory and hands-on practice.
- Recognizing the importance of experiential learning over theory alone.

#### Teamwork skills

- Understanding product development in Hackathon competitions.
- Learning fair task division among team members.
- Managing time and handling issues under high pressure.
- Recognizing effective communication as key to project success.

#### Soft skills

- Understanding confidence in learning, work, and life.
- Building confidence step by step through practice and experience.
- Proactively speaking up, asking questions, and sharing ideas for faster growth.

#### DevOps knowledge

- DevOps as process and culture, not just tools.
- Roles of CI/CD, Monitoring, Logging, and Automation in software development.
- Infrastructure, testing, and monitoring reduce deployment risks.

### Applying to Work

- Use Cloud Quest and Floci for structured AWS learning.
- Join Hackathons to improve teamwork and problem-solving.
- Practice communication and presentation for confidence in studies and interviews.
- Apply DevOps principles in personal projects: Git, CI/CD, automated testing.
- Build clear development processes to reduce deployment and operations risks.

### Event Experience

The event exposed me to valuable topics on AWS, DevOps, and skills needed for IT career growth. Speakers shared practical, relatable, and applicable experiences.

#### Learning from speakers

Speakers shared real experiences on AWS learning paths, Hackathons, building confidence, and DevOps in software projects. Beyond technical knowledge, soft skills and work mindset are equally important for IT engineers.

#### Technical experience

#### Lessons learned

- Hackathons are ideal for problem-solving, time management, and teamwork.
- Confidence is built through learning, experience, and continuous self-challenge.
- DevOps ensures quality, stability, and scalability of software systems.
- Software engineers need technical knowledge, soft skills, and professional work mindset for the digital era.
