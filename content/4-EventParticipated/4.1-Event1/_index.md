# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day 09-05-2026”

### Event Objectives

- Share effective learning and working methods in the AI era.
- Introduce how to leverage AI through Prompt Engineering.
- Provide career guidance for IT students in the context of rapid AI growth.
- Guide the application of AI in software development using the BMAD method (Business Method for AI Development).

### Speakers

- **Speaker 1:** Mr. Huynh Hoang Long — *Addicted to Learning Like You're Addicted to Social Media*.
- **Speaker 2:** Mr. Nguyen Tuan Thinh — *Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality*.
- **Speaker 3:** Mr. Khang — *AI-Ready Freshers*.
- **Speaker 4:** Ms. Thao — Software Developer at VIB (Vietnam International Bank), presenting the *BMAD – Method*.

### Key Highlights

#### Topic 1 – Hack your brain to love learning

- Explained how dopamine works and its impact on learning motivation.
- Analyzed why people are more drawn to TikTok, games, and social media than studying.
- Introduced habit-building methods: Gamification, random rewards, Learning Streaks, the 2-minute rule, and breaking goals into smaller steps.

#### Topic 2 – Prompt Engineering & AI

- Introduced how to write effective prompts for more accurate AI responses.
- Key prompt components: **Role**, **Context**, **Task**, **Input**, **Output Format**, **Constraints**.
- Demo of AI applications combined with AWS services: Amazon Bedrock, AWS Lambda, Amazon S3, Amazon Cognito, API Gateway, DynamoDB, CloudWatch.
- Shared experiences for reducing AI hallucination.

#### Topic 3 – AI and career orientation

- AI does not replace developers but improves productivity.
- The importance of foundational knowledge over learning many frameworks or tools.
- What employers value: problem-solving, "Why?" thinking, explaining solutions, integrity, and accountability.
- Encouraged students to use AI as an assistant rather than depending on it entirely.

#### Topic 4 – BMAD (Business Method for AI Development)

- Analyzed limitations of "Vibe Coding".
- Introduced BMAD for structured AI-assisted software development.
- Development process: Brainstorm → Specification → Architecture → Epic/Story/Task → Coding → Review → Testing.
- AI Agents simulating roles: Product Manager, Architect, Scrum Master, Developer, QA/Tester.
- Reducing hallucination by breaking work into smaller tasks and managing context effectively.

### Key Takeaways

#### Learning skills

- Understanding dopamine to build long-term learning motivation.
- Applying Gamification for effective study habits.
- Breaking goals into smaller steps to improve completion rates.

#### AI skills

- Designing clear, context-rich prompts.
- Combining AI with AWS services to build intelligent applications.
- Reducing hallucination through step-by-step prompting.

#### Software development skills

- Understanding documented, architecture-driven development processes.
- Recognizing the importance of documentation before coding.
- Splitting projects into Epics, Stories, and Tasks for AI-assisted work.

#### Career orientation

- Foundational knowledge matters more than using many tools.
- AI supports developers but cannot replace critical thinking.
- Always ask "Why?" before choosing a technical solution.
- Strengthening communication, analysis, and teamwork in software development.

### Applying to Work

- Use Gamification for daily learning plans.
- Apply Prompt Engineering to use ChatGPT and other AI tools effectively.
- Apply BMAD in personal projects with documentation before coding.
- Split projects into Epics and User Stories for easier task management.
- Use AI to support requirements analysis, architecture design, and code generation—not only direct coding.

### Event Experience

#### Learning from speakers

Speakers shared real experiences applying AI to learning, programming, and product development. Presentations on Prompt Engineering and BMAD showed that AI works best within a structured development process.

#### Technical experience

The workshop demonstrated:

- Quality prompt design.
- Software development workflows with AI Agents.
- Amazon Bedrock and AWS services in AI application building.
- Reducing hallucination through documentation and context management.

#### Career orientation

The session clarified skills employers expect today: beyond technical knowledge, thinking, problem-solving, communication, and working with AI are increasingly important.

#### Lessons learned

- Learning motivation can be built through habits and reward systems.
- Prompt Engineering is essential for effective AI use.
- AI boosts productivity when used correctly—it does not replace developers.
- Documentation is increasingly important in AI-assisted development.
- BMAD standardizes development, reduces errors, and improves maintainability.
- Foundational knowledge and problem-solving remain the most important skills for software engineers in the AI era.
